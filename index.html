📐 Formulas & Definitions
📊 Analytics Definitions
Win Rate
Win Rate = (Winning Trades ÷ Total Closed Trades) × 100
Percentage of your trades that were profitable. 50%+ is generally considered good, but must be evaluated alongside R:R.
Risk : Reward (R:R)
R:R = Average Win ÷ Average Loss
How much you gain vs how much you risk. A ratio of 2:1 means you make $2 for every $1 you risk. Even a 40% win rate can be profitable with a good R:R.
Profit Factor
Profit Factor = Gross Profit ÷ Gross Loss
Above 1.0 = profitable system. Above 1.5 = good. Above 2.0 = excellent. Below 1.0 = losing money overall.
ROI (Return on Investment)
ROI = (Net P&L ÷ Total Invested) × 100
How much you earned relative to what you invested. Expressed as a percentage.
Net P&L
Net P&L = Gross P&L − Fees
Your actual profit or loss after subtracting all brokerage fees and commissions.
Avg Entry Price (Dollar Cost Averaging)
Avg Entry = Total Cost ÷ Total Shares
Total Cost = Σ (Price × Qty) for each buy
When you buy the same stock multiple times at different prices, your average entry is the weighted average across all purchases.
Unrealized P&L
Unrealized P&L = (Current Price − Avg Entry) × Remaining Shares × Direction
P&L on positions still open. Direction = +1 for Long, −1 for Short. Not realized until you close.
🧮 Calculator Formulas
P&L Calculator
Gross P&L = (Exit − Entry) × Qty
Net P&L = Gross P&L − Fees
% Change = ((Exit − Entry) ÷ Entry) × 100
Basic profit/loss calculation for any trade. Use Long direction for buys, Short for sells/shorts.
Position Size — Mode 1 (Price-based)
Risk Amount = Balance × Risk%
Risk Per Share = |Entry − Stop Loss|
Shares = Risk Amount ÷ Risk Per Share
Position Size = Shares × Entry Price
Calculates exactly how many shares to buy so that if your stop loss is hit, you only lose your defined risk amount.
Position Size — Mode 2 (% SL)
Risk Amount = Balance × Risk%
Position Size = Risk Amount ÷ SL%
When you know your stop loss as a percentage from entry rather than an exact price.
Compound Growth
Balance(month) = Balance(prev) × (1 + r) + Deposit
where r = Annual Rate ÷ 12
Shows how your account grows month by month with a consistent return rate. The power of compounding means small consistent gains add up significantly over time.
📖 Trading Glossary
Long
Buying a stock expecting the price to go UP. You profit when price rises above your entry.
Short
Selling a stock you don't own, expecting the price to go DOWN. You profit when price falls below your entry.
Stop Loss (SL)
A price level where you exit a losing trade to prevent further losses. Essential for risk management.
Take Profit (TP)
A target price where you exit a winning trade to lock in profits.
Drawdown
The peak-to-trough decline in your account balance. Maximum drawdown is the largest drop from a high point.
Partial Close
Closing only a portion of your position (e.g. 50% of shares) to lock in some profit while keeping exposure.
